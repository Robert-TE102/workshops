6 Basic Starting Points: Type of thing, User flow, Hierarchy, Consistency, Balance, Simplicity#.
Contrast, Imagery, Typography and User feedback.

Type of Thing - What is the point of your material - Informs the User Goal, Informs the actions they can take.

User Flow - Map your users journey, from landing, to the end goal - Users have expectations - Wireframing can be helpful - Steer towards closure

Hierarchy - The most important thing is the most important thing - Define the order of importance of info/items on your page - Great marketing leads the user to only one option, because that's what we can realistically expect from them - Decisiveness, Contrast, Emphasis, Visual Hierarchy - White Space.

Consistency - Your site visuals should maintain consistency so that users understand what's going on - Maintain harmony between elements - Have reusable components, following a system/guidelines - Colour theme, Background, Text, Primary / Secondary, Accent, Semantic (Success/Error)
Be Consistent - Predictability.

Balance - Use delightful spacing, Alignment and Placement - Between page elements and elements themselves. -

Simplicity - Keep things clear and straightforward for the user. - Reduce thier cognitive load - Chunking: Group similar things/aspects of the UI. - Magic number 7: Allows the brain to compute the info it's seeing. - Reduce options. Tools: Less is more.

Contrast - for accessibility and emphasis - Not only does sensible and defined

Imagery - Photography and illustration - Add visual interest to your site. - Your content can be your imagery IE cards with images. - Creates emotional resonance with users. - Imagery is communication.

Typography - Refers to the way we select and arrange our 'Type'. - some "Dont haves" - too many, too much colour difference, too much bold/italic. - The type of work you should have -

User feedback - Gives a reaction for your users action. - Everyone click this google meet reaction .... - feedback keeps users engaged. - Lets them know when something is happening. - Make use of focus and interactive states - in HTML and CSS: Disabled, Default, Hover, Focus, Active - Give your users control -

General Tips - Test, Iterate, Prototype, Refine - Users already have a mental model of apps, sites and interactions; don't reinvent the wheel - You want to think like a user.
